AGEISM

●WHAT IS GENERATIONAL AGEISM?

Ageism includes discrimination toward both younger and older individuals. Discrimination based on generational cohorts can serve as a proxy for discrimination based on age and, therefore, can be argued as a form of ageism. Gendron et.al(2024). Generational ageism is a specific type of ageism that refers to prejudice and discrimination against people based on their generational affiliation. It's essentially judging someone based on broad stereotypes about their age group, rather than their individual merits. Ageism, a silent yet pervasive discrimination, often lurks in the shadows of our societal norms. In the context of the Philippines, a culture deeply rooted in respect for elders, the concept of ageism may seem paradoxical. How can a society so focused on venerating age also be guilty of discrimination based on it? The answer lies in the realization that respect should not be automatically granted based on age alone. In our culture, the saying "makisama ka sa matanda" (respect your elders) is a deeply ingrained principle. It is a beautiful tradition that upholds the wisdom and experience of those who came before us. However, what happens when age becomes a shield for incompetence, a justification for mistreatment, or a barrier to opportunities for the young? This dichotomy demands our attention and introspection. While it is crucial to honor the wisdom of the elderly, it is equally important to recognize that age does not inherently guarantee respectability or competence. Respect should be earned through actions, character, and integrity, regardless of age. The idea that "age is just a number" is not a call to dismiss the wisdom of the elderly but rather a plea to view individuals holistically. Age should not define a person's worth, capabilities, or contributions to society. We must challenge the notion that older individuals are less capable or relevant in the workforce, in social settings, or in decision-making roles. Moreover, we must recognize that ageism affects everyone. The images of older individuals struggling to find employment or being passed over for promotion due to their age are poignant reminders of this reality. Yet, ageism also impacts the young, with stereotypes of inexperience or immaturity limiting their opportunities for growth and development. As we champion campaigns for gender rights, human rights, and animal rights, let us not forget the battle against ageism. It is a multifaceted issue that requires a shift in mindset and policies. We must create inclusive environments where individuals are valued for their skills, ideas, and contributions, regardless of their age. Reflecting on ageism in our society prompts us to consider how we view and treat each other. It challenges us to confront biases and stereotypes that limit individuals based on their age. It calls for a reevaluation of our cultural norms to ensure that respect is earned through actions, not just years lived. In spreading awareness about ageism, we pave the way for a more equitable and inclusive society. Let us raise our voices, challenge the status quo, and strive to create a world where age truly becomes just a number—a number that does not dictate one's worth or potential.
